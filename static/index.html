How to Read

A breakdown of every section on your stock cards, top to bottom.

Overall Score

A number from 0–100 representing the stock's overall investment attractiveness based on five weighted factors.

80–100 Strong signal

60–79 Moderate opportunity

40–59 Neutral / wait

0–39 Caution

Risk & Confidence

Risk level (Low / Medium / High) reflects how volatile the stock is — how much the price might swing up or down.

Confidence % shows how complete the underlying data was. Lower confidence means fewer data points were available for the analysis.

Time horizon is the suggested holding period — short-term (days/weeks) or long-term (months/years).

Score Breakdown

Five bars make up the overall score, each weighted differently:

Momentum (20pts) — Is the price trending upward? Measured using RSI and recent returns.

Growth (25pts) — Are revenues and earnings expanding? Higher weight as it drives long-term value.

Quality (20pts) — News sentiment and analyst outlook. Positive press and upgrades score higher.

Valuation (20pts) — Is the stock priced fairly compared to its sector? Low P/E relative to peers scores higher.

Stability (15pts) — Consistency of price behaviour over time. Less erratic = higher score.

Price Charts

1-Year chart shows daily closing prices over the past 12 months — useful for spotting recent momentum or a downturn.

5-Year chart shows weekly prices over 5 years — useful for understanding the broader trend and whether dips are temporary.

A stock rising on both charts is generally a stronger signal than one only rising recently.

Monte Carlo Simulation

Runs 1,000 simulated future price paths based on the stock's historical daily returns and volatility.

Bear — the worst 10% of simulated outcomes.

Median — the middle outcome (50th percentile).

Bull — the best 10% of simulated outcomes.

The % gain probability is how often the simulation ended in profit. Above 60% is generally positive.

This is not a prediction — it models probability based on past behaviour only.

Model Inputs

The raw financial signals feeding the score. Key ones to know:

RSI — Relative Strength Index. Above 70 = overbought (may fall), below 30 = oversold (may rise).

P/E Ratio — Price-to-Earnings. Lower than the sector average suggests better value.

Revenue growth — Year-over-year sales increase. Positive and accelerating is ideal.

Volatility — How much the daily price moves. Higher volatility = more risk and reward.

Reasoning

A plain-English AI summary explaining the four key factors behind the score:

Situation — What's happening with this stock right now.

Key signal — The single most important data point driving the score.

Risk — The main thing that could go wrong.

Why now — Whether the current timing looks favourable or not.

External Links

Yahoo, Google, and Sharesies links open that stock's page on those sites for independent price checking, news, and buying.
